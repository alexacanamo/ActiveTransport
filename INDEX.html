Definition

Active transport moves molecules across cell membranes against their concentration gradient (low to high), using energy (ATP).

Sodium-Potassium Pump

The sodium-potassium pump is a protein in the cell membrane that uses energy (ATP) to move sodium ions out of the cell and potassium ions into the cell, helping maintain proper cell function.

Bulk Transport

Bulk transport is the movement of large molecules or particles into or out of the cell using energy, through processes like endocytosis and exocytosis.
