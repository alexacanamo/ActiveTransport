Learning Active Transport, the Smart Way

Types of Bulk Transport

Endocytosis

Endocytosis is the process by which cells take in large molecules, particles, or even other cells by engulfing them with their cell membrane, forming a vesicle inside the cell. This is how cells absorb nutrients and other essential substances.

Exocytosis

Exocytosis is the process by which cells expel materials in vesicles that fuse with the cell membrane, releasing their contents outside the cell. This is important for removing waste and secreting substances like hormones.

Types of Endocytosis

Phagocytosis

Known as "cell eating," phagocytosis is when the cell engulfs large particles or even other cells, forming a phagosome. This is common in immune cells like macrophages.

Pinocytosis

Called "cell drinking," pinocytosis is when the cell engulfs droplets of extracellular fluid, taking in dissolved substances in small vesicles.

Receptor-Mediated Endocytosis

This highly specific process uses receptors on the cell surface to target and engulf particular molecules, such as hormones or nutrients, into coated vesicles.
