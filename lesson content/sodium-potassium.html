Definition

The sodium-potassium pump is a membrane protein that uses energy from ATP to move sodium ions out of the cell and potassium ions into the cell, both against their concentration gradients. This process is essential for maintaining the cell's electrochemical balance and is vital for nerve impulse transmission and muscle contraction.

How it Works

The pump binds three sodium ions inside the cell and, using energy from ATP, changes shape to release them outside. It then binds two potassium ions from outside and transports them into the cell. This cycle repeats, maintaining high potassium and low sodium concentrations inside the cell, which is crucial for many cellular functions.
